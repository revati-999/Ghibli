CHARACTER DESIGN

Sophie Hatter

Sophie Hatter is the eldest of three sisters in the land of Ingary, where fairy-tale tropes are a part of everyday life. Sophie leads an uneventful life until she is transformed into an old woman by a Witch. This curse leads her into an adventure involving a mysterious moving castle owned by the feared wizard Howl.

Howl

Howl is a charismatic and enigmatic wizard with a flair for dramatics and aversion to conflict. Howl is known for his striking appearance and magical prowess. He operates a moving castle, which is as fantastical and eccentric as Howl himself. The castle reflects his chaotic and transient life, changing just like his personas.

Markl and Calcifer

Markl, Howl's eager young apprentice, is keen to master the magical arts amid the wonders of the moving castle. He helps manage the day-to-day operations of Howl's moving castle and looks up to Howl as a mentor and guardian

Lettie and Honey Hatter

Honey Hatter is the mother of Sophie and Lettie Hatter. Honey is pragmatic and owns a hat shop in Market Chipping, where Sophie initially works.

Lettie is Sophie Hatter's younger sister who is quiet cheerful and outgoing. She works in a bakery in their hometown of Market Chipping. Lettie is supportive and caring towards Sophie, offering encouragement and warmth

Building the World

Howl's Moving Castle is an animation fantasy movie written and directed by Hayao Miyazaki in 2004. Influenced by Miyazaki's opposition to the United States' invasion of Iraq in 2003, Howl's Moving Castle contains strongly anti-war themes. It also explores the theme of old age, depicting age positively as something which grants the protagonist freedom. The film also carries messages about the value of compassion. In 2013 Miyazaki said the film was his favorite creation, explaining "I wanted to convey the message that life is worth living, and I don't think that's changed."
